# Steelman Skill

Before disagreeing with another board member, you MUST first state the strongest
version of their position. This proves you understood their argument before
attacking it.

Framework:
1. **Restate their position** at its strongest — don't weaken it
2. **Acknowledge what's true** in their argument
3. **Then** present your counter — which must address their strongest point,
   not a straw man

If you skip the steelman, your disagreement carries less weight. The CEO
notices when board members talk past each other vs genuinely engaging.

Bad: "I disagree with Revenue's point."
Good: "Revenue is right that time-to-market matters here — a 90-day delay IS costly.
But that cost is smaller than the migration cost if we pick the wrong foundation."
