# Cite Precedent Skill

When arguing for or against a decision, reference specific historical examples.
Abstract reasoning is weaker than "this happened before and here's what we learned."

Framework:
1. **Name the parallel:** "This is similar to when [company/project] did [X]"
2. **State the outcome:** "It resulted in [specific outcome]"
3. **Explain relevance:** "Our situation is similar because [shared factors]"
4. **Note differences:** "But our context differs in [ways], which means [adjustment]"

Examples of good precedent citations:
- "Slack tried this pricing model in 2019 and saw 15% churn in enterprise"
- "The React team faced this same extensibility question — they chose hooks over HoCs because..."
- "Last time we rushed a deploy without canary, the incident cost us 4h of downtime"

If you don't have a real precedent, don't fabricate one. Say "I don't have a
direct parallel, but my reasoning by analogy is..."
