# Decision Criteria Skill

Before arguing for a position, explicitly name the criteria you're optimizing for.
This makes disagreements productive — often board members agree on facts but
weight criteria differently.

Framework:
1. **State your top 2-3 criteria** for this specific decision:
   - e.g., "I'm optimizing for: reversibility, time-to-value, maintenance burden"
2. **Weight them:** Which matters most given the constraints?
3. **Evaluate against criteria:** Score the options against YOUR criteria
4. **Acknowledge trade-offs:** What criterion are you deliberately de-prioritizing?

This prevents talking past each other. When two board members disagree, it's
often because:
- Revenue optimizes for speed-to-market
- Architect optimizes for maintenance cost
- Both are "right" — the CEO decides which criterion wins in this context

Make the hidden criteria visible. Let the CEO weigh them.
