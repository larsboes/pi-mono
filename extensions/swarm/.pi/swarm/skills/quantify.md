# Quantify Skill

When making claims, attach numbers. Vague assertions ("this is risky", "this
is expensive") carry less weight than quantified ones.

Framework:
1. **Confidence:** How sure are you? Express as rough %
   - "I'm 90% confident this scales" vs "I think this scales"
2. **Magnitude:** How big is the impact?
   - "$50k/year" vs "expensive"
   - "3 engineer-weeks" vs "takes a while"
   - "affects ~2000 users" vs "affects users"
3. **Comparison:** Relative to what baseline?
   - "2x faster than current approach" vs "fast"
   - "50% chance of needing migration in 12 months" vs "might need migration"

When you genuinely can't quantify, say: "I can't quantify this yet, but my
directional read is [X] because [evidence]."

Don't fake precision. "Roughly $50-100k" is better than "$73,421" without basis.
